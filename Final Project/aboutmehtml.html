About the Creators:


Anusha Palanivelu

Anusha Palanivelu, a 16 year old living in San Ramon, has a talent in voicing
her unique and thoughful opinions, motivated by her remarkable interest to
interact with her peers. She contributed greatly to this project through her
in-depth research on the presidential candidates, following with presenting the
various views on policies and helping write the game script. Anusha was introduced
to the world of code from the event Hackathon, eager to manipulate something
to turn it into something new. She joined Girls Who Code and is really grateful
for the experience she recieve on the exposure for coding.


“Try everything you never know what could happen” - Anusha Palanivelu

Gwen Patwardhan

Gwendolyn Patwardhan, a 16 year old living in San Jose, greatly contributed to this project by
designing and illustrating the characters for our game. Additionally, she extensively worked on
the research of the presidential candidates, the policies and scriptwriting. Her contribution to this
project has greatly impacted the condition of this project. Gwen’s first experience with coding
was in 4th grade when she found a course on Khan Academy and decided she wanted to try it
out. At first, when she started she really enjoyed it but after a while, she got bored and stopped.
But then after her sister participated in Girls Who Code, she decided she wanted to do the same
and try it out. Her advice for everyone is to try everything because especially for coding you can
combine it with almost anything. This experience has taught Gwen amazing sisterhood and a lot
more about coding.


“Try everything with an open mind but don't be afraid to fail” - Gwendolyn Patwardhan
Hannah Zhuang

Hannah Zhuang, an upcoming junior in highschool living in Richmond, her curiosity for the
fascinating world of technology led her to explore this complex network in hopes of contributing
her own unique ideas. She loves to bring her bubbly personality into the conversation,
spreading enthusiasm among her peers, and challenging herself to develop various skills to
improve her work ethic. Hannah contributed to the project by partaking in the web development
team, helping code and design the pages for the project website. She was introduced to the
GWC program through school, finding that this was the perfect opportunity to discover the
various career paths to pursue.This program has left Hannah was an amazing sisterhood and
the determination to find her burning passion to create.


"Don't be afraid to explore if you're curious"- Hannah Zhuang
Nura Sinan

Nura Sinan is a 16 year from Danville. Her contributions to the development of the website and
the script really ensured the team worked as efficiently as possible. Her first encounter with
coding was in 7th grade, the experience left a sour impression on her. However, when she
learned of Girls Who Code years later, she decided to give it another shot. Though she endured
her fair share of trials and tribulations throughout the program, she developed strong bonds with
her peers and her love for coding grew. She considers herself lucky for being able to experience
a program that nurtured such a healthy environment to learn more about the field of computer
science and her own capabilities.

"Don't give up even if you really want to"- Nura Sinan
Sara Wu

Sara Wu, an incoming high school Junior from Concord, lent her talents as a proficient coder to
the construction of the game and the basic structuring of the website. She has always
possessed an intrest in the field of computer science and her skills for communication helped to
organize the infrastructure of the project. Her first experience with coding was in school with

“Do what you love and don’t be afraid to do it” -Sara Wu
